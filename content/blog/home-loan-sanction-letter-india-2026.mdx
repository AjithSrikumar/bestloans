## What is a Home Loan Sanction Letter?

A home loan sanction letter (also called a sanction order or approval letter) is a formal document issued by a bank or HFC confirming that your home loan application has been approved. It specifies the exact terms on which the lender is willing to disburse the loan.

**Crucially — it is NOT the same as disbursement.** The sanction letter simply confirms your eligibility; disbursement happens only after all conditions in the letter are met.

## 12 Key Things to Verify in Your Sanction Letter

| # | What to Check | Why It Matters |
|---|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Approved loan amount** | Confirm this matches what you applied for. If lower, verify if shortfall can be covered |
| 2 | **Interest rate** | Note the exact rate (e.g., EBLR = 5.25% + 2.00% spread = 7.25% p.a.) — verify no errors |
| 3 | **Rate type** | Floating (EBLR/RLLR/MCLR-linked) or fixed — most April 2026 home loans are floating |
| 4 | **Rate reset mechanism** | Quarterly reset is standard for EBLR-linked loans (25th March, June, September, December) |
| 5 | **Loan tenure** | Cross-check with the tenure you requested |
| 6 | **EMI amount** | Calculate independently using a loan calculator and cross-check |
| 7 | **Conditions precedent** | List of things you must do before disbursement — property registration, insurance, specific documents |
| 8 | **Sanction validity** | Typically 3–6 months — disbursement must happen within this window |
| 9 | **Processing fee** | Amount and payment timeline |
| 10 | **Prepayment/foreclosure terms** | Should state **zero charges** for floating-rate individual borrowers |
| 11 | **Property details** | Confirm the property description matches exactly — any mismatch will delay disbursement |
| 12 | **Penal interest on EMI bounce** | Typically 1%–2% per month on the overdue amount — note for financial planning |

## Can Terms Be Changed After the Sanction Letter?

**Within the sanction validity:** Yes — you can request modifications to tenure or EMI structure, though the bank may need to re-evaluate.

**After sanction expires:** A fresh application may be required, with a fresh credit and property check.

**Interest rate:** The rate is fixed at the time of **disbursement**, not at the time of sanction. If the RBI changes the repo rate between sanction and disbursement, the rate in your first EMI may differ from what appears in the sanction letter.

## What Happens If You Don't Take the Loan After Sanction?

- Processing fees already paid are typically **non-refundable**
- No credit impact from the sanction itself
- You can decline the loan at any stage
- The hard CIBIL inquiry already made during processing remains on your report for 24 months (though it stops affecting your score after a few months)
