## Why CIBIL Score is More Important Than Ever in 2026

In 2026, all major Indian lenders use **risk-based pricing** — your interest rate is directly tied to your CIBIL score band.

A score of 750 versus 700 can mean a rate difference of 0.25%, and on a Rs. 50 lakh, 20-year loan, that 0.25% gap adds up to **Rs. 1.7 lakh in total extra interest**. A score of 800+ versus 750 can save another Rs. 80,000–1,00,000.

## CIBIL Score Bands and Their Home Loan Impact — April 2026

| Score Band | Rate Range | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 800–900 (Excellent) | 7.10%–7.25% | Fastest approval; best LTV; pre-approved offers possible |
| 750–799 (Very Good) | 7.25%–7.60% | Smooth approval; standard LTV norms |
| 700–749 (Good) | 7.60%–8.25% | Approved at most banks; additional income docs may be requested |
| 650–699 (Fair) | 8.25%–9.50%+ | Mostly NBFCs/HFCs; higher down payment expected |
| 600–649 (Poor) | Very high | Most banks reject; some HFCs consider with co-applicant + 30%+ down payment |
| Below 600 (Very Poor) | N/A | Near-universal rejection; rebuild over 6–12 months |

## What Hurts Your Score the Most — 2026 Reality

- **Missed or delayed EMI/credit card payment** — impact: minus 50–100 points from a single miss
- **Credit card utilisation above 30%** — impact: minus 20–40 points if consistently high
- **Multiple loan applications in 90 days** — each hard inquiry drops score by 5–10 points
- **Written-off or settled loans** — among the most damaging entries; can persist for 7 years
- **Closing old credit card accounts** — reduces credit history length and available credit

## 90-Day CIBIL Score Improvement Plan

### Month 1: Stop the Bleeding
- Clear all overdue amounts immediately — even partial payments stop the bleeding
- Set up ECS/NACH auto-debit for every EMI and card payment to prevent future misses
- Pull your free credit report from CIBIL, Experian, and CRIF (one free per year from each bureau)
- Dispute any errors immediately through the bureau's online portal

### Month 2: Reduce Utilisation and Inquiries
- Pay down credit card balances to **below 30%** of each card's credit limit
- Request a credit limit increase on existing cards (lowers utilisation ratio without new debt)
- **Do NOT apply for any new loans or credit cards** — every application triggers a hard inquiry
- If you have a defaulted loan, negotiate a settlement and get the NOC; ensure lender reports 'Settled'

### Month 3: Review and Apply
- Re-check your CIBIL score; maintain all on-time payments
- If a previously settled loan is still showing 'Written Off', file a dispute with CIBIL referencing the NOC
- If score has crossed 700, begin comparing home loan lenders
- Above 750, you are in the preferred borrower category

## Low CIBIL? These Workarounds Exist in 2026

1. **Co-applicant with high CIBIL** — lenders evaluate both scores; a 750+ co-applicant can overcome a 680 primary score
2. **Larger down payment (30%+)** — lower LTV reduces lender risk and improves approval odds
3. **Approach HFCs and NBFCs** — more flexible underwriting than scheduled commercial banks
4. **Guarantor arrangement** — guarantor is fully liable if you default; use only if genuinely needed
