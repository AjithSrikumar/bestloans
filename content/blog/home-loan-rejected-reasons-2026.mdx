## How Common is Home Loan Rejection in India?

Approximately **20–25% of all home loan applications** in India face rejection at some stage, according to industry estimates. The good news: nearly every rejection reason is fixable.

Banks evaluate three broad areas — your **creditworthiness** (CIBIL score, repayment history), your **repayment capacity** (income, existing obligations), and the **property itself** (legal title, valuation, approved layout). A weakness in any of these triggers rejection.

---

## Reason 1: Low CIBIL Score (Most Common)

A CIBIL score below 650 is a red flag at most banks. In April 2026, the minimum acceptable score at major PSU banks is 650–700; private banks prefer 700–750.

**The Fix:** Check your credit report for errors (dispute them immediately at cibil.com). Pay all overdue bills. Reduce credit card utilisation below 30%. Wait 90 days after improvement before reapplying.

---

## Reason 2: High FOIR — Too Many Existing EMIs

FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) measures your existing monthly EMIs as a percentage of gross income. If your existing EMIs leave less than 40–50% of income free, the bank cannot fit in the new home loan EMI.

**The Fix:** Close or prepay personal loans, two-wheeler loans, or credit card dues before applying. Adding a co-applicant with clean income is the fastest alternative fix.

---

## Reason 3: Insufficient Income for the Loan Amount

Banks apply income multiplier methods. If your salary supports a Rs. 35 lakh loan but you applied for Rs. 60 lakh, the application is rejected.

**The Fix:** Apply for the amount your income supports, or add a co-applicant. You can also reduce the loan amount and increase the down payment.

---

## Reason 4: Property Title Defects

This is the most frustrating reason because it is not your fault. If the property changed hands multiple times and any link in the sale deed chain is missing, the bank's legal team will reject it. Name mismatches between the sale deed and revenue records also cause rejection.

**The Fix:** Ask the seller to provide all link documents and the complete title chain. Ensure names are consistent across all records.

---

## Reason 5: Non-RERA Project

As of April 2026, all regulated lenders mandatorily check RERA registration before sanctioning home loans for under-construction properties. An unregistered project = automatic rejection.

**The Fix:** Ensure the project has a valid RERA registration number. Verify it on your state's RERA portal. Alternatively, choose a RERA-registered project.

---

## Reason 6: Unstable Employment History

Frequent job switches (3+ changes in 2 years), probation period employment, or gaps between jobs create doubt about income continuity. Self-employed applicants with business vintage under 3 years also face scrutiny.

**The Fix:** Wait until you have completed at least 12 months at your current employer before applying. For self-employed, ensure 3 years of consistent filed ITRs.

---

## Reason 7: Credit Report Errors

You may have a clean repayment history but errors in your credit report — duplicate accounts, incorrect defaults, loans you guaranteed for others that defaulted — can drag your score down unfairly.

**The Fix:** Pull reports from all four bureaus (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF High Mark). Dispute errors with the bureau and the reporting lender separately. Keep all correspondence records.

---

## Reason 8: Guarantor on a Defaulted Loan

If you have guaranteed a loan for a family member or friend who has defaulted, that default appears on your credit profile. Banks can — and do — reject home loan applications because of this.

**The Fix:** Encourage the primary borrower to pay the dues or settle the loan. Once the account is closed and reported correctly, your profile improves.

---

## Reason 9: Property Valuation Lower Than Sale Price

The bank sanctions only up to the LTV percentage of the property's valuation (not the sale price). If the bank values the property at Rs. 55 lakh but you agreed to pay Rs. 65 lakh, the loan is capped at 80% of Rs. 55 lakh = **Rs. 44 lakh**.

**The Fix:** Negotiate the sale price down to match valuation, or arrange the difference from personal funds.

---

## Reason 10: Age-Related Tenure Restrictions

If you are 55 years old and applying for a 25-year loan, the bank will limit the tenure to 10 years (to ensure repayment before 65). The higher EMI on the shorter tenure may push FOIR beyond acceptable levels.

**The Fix:** Add a younger co-applicant (adult child) to extend the eligible tenure and reduce effective EMI.

## What to Do Immediately After Rejection

1. Ask the lender for the specific reason in writing — you have the right to know
2. Do **NOT** apply to multiple lenders simultaneously — each hard inquiry drops your CIBIL score 5–10 points
