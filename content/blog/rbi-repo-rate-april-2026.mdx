## RBI MPC Decision — 8 April 2026

The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC), chaired by Governor Sanjay Malhotra, voted to hold the repo rate at **5.25%** on 8 April 2026. This is the second consecutive hold following the 25 bps cut in December 2025 that brought the rate from 5.50% to 5.25%.

The RBI cited heightened geopolitical uncertainty in West Asia, elevated energy price pressures, and upside inflation risks as reasons to pause.

## Why This Matters for Your Home Loan

For the ~95% of home loan borrowers on floating / EBLR-linked rates, **no immediate EMI change occurs** — the rate pause freezes your EMI at post-December 2025 levels.

Banks using EBLR (External Benchmark Linked Rate) must transmit any repo change within 3 months; since there is no change, your EMI stays put.

## What Borrowers Should Do

- **If on EBLR/RLLR:** your rate is already the best available — no action needed
- **If on MCLR:** check if converting to EBLR saves money after the conversion fee
- **New applicants:** this stable rate window is an excellent time to compare and lock in
- **Existing borrowers with rates above 8.5%:** evaluate a balance transfer — you could save lakhs
