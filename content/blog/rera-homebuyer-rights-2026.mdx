## RERA in 2026: The Regulatory Backbone

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 — RERA — is now in its tenth year of operation. By April 2026, **all major states have fully functional RERA authorities** with active project registrations, complaint mechanisms, and enforcement capabilities.

For homebuyers, RERA represents the most significant consumer protection since the Consumer Protection Act.

---

## Your 6 Core Rights Under RERA — April 2026

### 1. Right to Timely Possession
Builder must deliver by the committed date. **Delay compensation:** interest at SBI's highest MCLR + 2% per month of delay, until possession is handed over.

### 2. Right Against Cost Escalation
Builder cannot increase the price after signing the agreement — **any quote given at booking is binding under RERA**.

### 3. Right to Structural Defect Warranty
**5 years from possession** for any structural defect — builder must repair at zero cost.

### 4. Right to Accurate Carpet Area
Carpet area must be disclosed per RERA's definition (inner wall measurements only, excluding common areas) — no vague 'super built-up area' pricing.

### 5. Right to Escrow Protection
**70% of all buyer funds** must be held in a dedicated RERA escrow account — withdrawals permitted only linked to construction progress, certified by an engineer and architect.

### 6. Right Against Misleading Advertisement
Any brochure, map, or specification shown during booking is legally binding — **cannot be changed post-agreement**.

## How to Check a Project's RERA Status — April 2026

| State | RERA Portal |
|-------|------------|
| Maharashtra | maharera.mahaonline.gov.in |
| Karnataka | rera.karnataka.gov.in |
| Telangana | rera.telangana.gov.in |
| Delhi | dda.delhi.gov.in/rera |
| UP | up-rera.in |
| Haryana | haryanarera.gov.in |

On each portal, search by project name, builder name, or RERA registration number. Check: registration validity, approved plans, construction progress updates, any complaints filed against the project, and agent registration.

## How to File a RERA Complaint in 2026

**Step 1:** Document your grievance with supporting evidence (booking receipt, payment receipts, builder correspondence, original agreement)

**Step 2:** File complaint online on your state's RERA portal — include the project's RERA registration number

**Step 3:** Pay the nominal filing fee (typically Rs. 500–1,000)

**Step 4:** RERA adjudicator schedules a hearing within 30 days

**Step 5:** Order issued within 60 days

**Step 6:** If builder does not comply, file execution petition — RERA has powers to direct revenue recovery

## RERA and Home Loans: The Critical Link

As of April 2026, banks **mandatorily check RERA registration** before sanctioning home loans for under-construction properties. An unregistered project cannot get bank financing from any regulated lender.

RERA registration also signals: approved building plans, builder's commitment to statutory compliance, and active regulatory oversight — all factors that reduce bank risk and speed up your loan sanction.
