## ITR Declaration for AY 2026–27 (FY 2025–26): Starting Point

Declaring a home loan in the income tax return for AY 2026–27 requires getting two things right:
1. Correctly classifying and reporting the property under the **Income from House Property** schedule
2. Claiming the correct deductions — all of which depend critically on whether you opt for the **old or new tax regime**

---

## Regime Choice: The Most Important First Step

| Regime | Section 24(b) | Section 80C | Section 80EEA |
|--------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| **New Regime** (default in 2026) | ❌ Not allowed for SOP | ❌ Not allowed | ❌ Not allowed |
| **Old Regime** (requires active opt-in) | ✅ Up to Rs. 2,00,000 | ✅ Up to Rs. 1,50,000 | ✅ Up to Rs. 1,50,000 (if eligible) |

> The only home loan benefit available in the new regime: interest on a **let-out/rented property** can be deducted against rental income.

---

## Step-by-Step ITR Filing for Home Loan Borrowers

**Step 1:** Login to incometax.gov.in → File ITR → Select correct ITR form
- ITR-1: salaried borrowers with self-occupied property
- ITR-2: let-out property or multiple properties

**Step 2:** Select tax regime — choose **old regime** to claim home loan deductions

**Step 3:** Fill 'Schedule HP' (House Property): enter property details, annual value, municipal taxes paid, 30% standard deduction (for let-out), interest under Section 24(b)

**Step 4:** Fill 'Schedule VI-A': enter principal repayment under Section 80C (combined with other 80C investments, up to Rs. 1.5 lakh ceiling)

**Step 5:** If eligible for 80EEA, enter in the specific 80EEA row in Schedule VI-A

**Step 6:** For salaried borrowers: ensure Form 12BB submitted to employer at the start of year is consistent with ITR claims

## Documents to Keep Ready for AY 2026–27

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Annual interest certificate from lender | Shows interest and principal split for FY 2025–26 |
| Loan account statement | Confirms principal repaid and interest paid |
| Possession letter / completion certificate | Establishes the 5-year construction completion condition |
| Registered sale deed + stamp duty receipt | For 80C claim in year of purchase |
| Original loan sanction letter | For 80EEA: confirms sanction date and property value |

---

## Common Filing Errors — AY 2026–27

1. **Claiming SOP interest in the new regime** — flagged by the IT system and creates demand notices

2. **Wrong property classification:** treating let-out property as SOP to claim Rs. 2 lakh interest cap incorrectly

3. **Missing pre-construction interest instalment:** many borrowers forget to claim the annual 1/5th instalment

4. **80EEA without verifying sanction date eligibility:** 80EEA applies only to loans sanctioned **1 April 2019 – 31 March 2022**
