## Pre-EMI vs Full EMI: The Core Difference

For under-construction properties where the loan is disbursed in tranches, banks offer two EMI options:

- **Pre-EMI:** Interest-only payments during construction, full EMI after possession
- **Full EMI:** Principal + interest from the first disbursement itself

The difference is significant — in both monthly outgo and total interest paid over the loan life.

---

## How Pre-EMI Works

Pre-EMI = **Interest only** on the amount disbursed so far.

**Example:** Loan of Rs. 50 lakh, first disbursement of Rs. 10 lakh at 7.25% p.a.
- Pre-EMI for first tranche = Rs. 10,00,000 × 7.25% ÷ 12 = **Rs. 6,042/month**
- This continues and increases as more tranches are disbursed
- After full disbursement and possession, the **full EMI of Rs. 39,672/month** begins
- The 20-year loan tenure starts from this point (not from first disbursement)

---

## How Full EMI Works

Full EMI = **Principal + Interest** on the disbursed amount from first disbursement.

**Using the same example:** First disbursement of Rs. 10 lakh:
- Full EMI = Rs. 10 lakh loan, 20-year tenure → **Rs. 7,934/month**
- As more tranches are disbursed, the full EMI increases proportionally
- **The advantage:** Principal repayment starts immediately, reducing total interest

---

## Total Cost Comparison — Which Costs More?

For a **Rs. 50 lakh loan at 7.25% with a 3-year construction period** (disbursed uniformly over 3 years):

| Option | Total Outgo During Construction | Overall Outcome |
|--------|--------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Pre-EMI** | Approximately Rs. 6.85 lakh (interest only) | **20-year tenure starts AFTER possession** |
| **Full EMI** | Approximately Rs. 9.8–10.2 lakh | **Principal repayment started from Day 1** |

Pre-EMI appears cheaper during construction. **BUT:** with pre-EMI, the 20-year full tenure starts after possession. With full EMI, it started from first disbursement. **Full EMI saves approximately Rs. 3–5 lakh** in total interest over the life of the loan despite higher payments during construction.

## Tax Treatment of Pre-EMI vs Full EMI

| Tax Aspect | Pre-EMI | Full EMI |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Interest during construction** | NOT deductible in the year paid. After possession, split into 5 equal instalments claimable under Section 24(b) over 5 years | Deductible under Section 24(b) from the year of payment |
| **Principal** | Not applicable (no principal paid) | Qualifies under Section 80C from Year 1 |
| **Annual cap** | Rs. 2 lakh (SOP) under Section 24(b) | Same Rs. 2 lakh annual cap |

> **Note:** Under the new tax regime (default in 2026), Section 80C and Section 24(b) deductions for home loans are not available.

---

## Which Should You Choose in 2026?

**Choose Full EMI if:**
- Your income can support the higher outgo during construction
- You want to minimise total interest paid
- You prefer tax benefits earlier (under old tax regime)

**Choose Pre-EMI if:**
- Your income is under pressure during construction (e.g., you are also paying rent)
- You cannot afford the higher Full EMI during construction
- You have another property under renovation that limits cash flow

For most financially comfortable borrowers in 2026, **Full EMI is the better financial choice** over the full loan lifetime.
