## What is a Home Loan Overdraft (OD) Facility?

A home loan overdraft facility is a hybrid product that combines a home loan with an overdraft (current/savings) account. Your outstanding balance at any point is reduced by any surplus funds parked in the linked OD account. **Interest is calculated on the 'net' outstanding** (loan balance minus OD balance) rather than the full loan balance.

Major offerings: **SBI Maxgain** and **HDFC Home Saver.**

---

## How the Interest Saving Works — Worked Example

**Loan: Rs. 50 lakh at 7.25% p.a.**

| Scenario | Daily Interest | Monthly Interest |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Standard loan** (no OD) | Rs. 993/day | Rs. 29,790 |
| **With OD** (Rs. 5 lakh parked) | Rs. 894/day | Rs. 26,811 |
| **Monthly saving** | — | **Rs. 2,979** |

**Annual saving:** Rs. 35,753 on Rs. 5 lakh parked — an effective return of **7.25% on your idle savings**, better than most savings accounts and comparable to FDs.

## SBI Maxgain — April 2026

SBI's overdraft-linked home loan.

- **Linked account:** SB/current account
- **Withdrawals:** Withdraw any amount parked (up to the original limit) — no restriction on number of withdrawals
- **EMI:** Continues normally; the OD balance only reduces interest charged
- **Rates:** Same as SBI regular home loan — from 7.25% p.a.
- **Eligibility:** Same as standard SBI home loan
- **Key advantage:** You can withdraw parked funds anytime — unlike prepayment which is permanent

---

## HDFC Home Saver — April 2026

HDFC's equivalent OD-linked home loan product.

- **Features:** Overdraft account linked to home loan; deposit surplus, withdraw as needed
- **Interest:** Charged on net outstanding only
- **Rates:** Slightly higher than HDFC standard home loan — the OD feature carries a small premium (approximately 0.05%–0.10% over standard rate)
- **Key advantage:** Flexibility to use deposited funds for emergencies while still saving interest

---

## Who Benefits Most From a Home Loan OD Facility?

- **Salaried borrowers** with irregular income or variable bonuses — park money when available, withdraw when needed
- **Self-employed borrowers** with fluctuating cash flows — money can move in and out of the OD account freely
- **Borrowers maintaining large liquid savings** (Rs. 3 lakh+) — the interest saving on this parked amount exceeds FD rates
- **Borrowers who want prepayment flexibility** without irrevocably reducing their loan

---

## When a Standard Home Loan is Better

**When you have no surplus funds to park:** The OD premium rate (0.05%–0.10% higher) is a net negative if you don't use the facility.

**When you are a disciplined prepayer:** If you make systematic annual prepayments and have no need to re-draw funds, a standard loan with zero prepayment charges achieves the same result at a lower rate.
