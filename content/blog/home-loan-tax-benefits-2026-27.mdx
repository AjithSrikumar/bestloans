## Home Loan Tax Benefits in FY 2026–27: The Big Picture

For FY 2026–27 (April 2026 to March 2027), home loan tax deductions remain available — but **exclusively under the old tax regime**. The new tax regime, which is the default in India since Budget 2023, does NOT allow deductions under Section 24(b) for self-occupied property, nor Section 80C for principal repayment.

Choosing your regime is therefore the most important tax decision for any home loan borrower.

---

## Section 24(b) — Interest Deduction

- **Maximum deduction:** Rs. 2,00,000 per year for self-occupied property (if the loan is for acquisition or construction and is completed within 5 years from sanction)
- If construction is not completed within 5 years, or the loan is for repairs/renovation, the limit drops to Rs. 30,000
- **For let-out or deemed let-out property:** interest deduction has NO upper limit — the actual interest paid is fully deductible against rental income. However, any resulting loss can only be set off against other income up to Rs. 2,00,000 per year (balance carried forward up to 8 years)

---

## Section 80C — Principal Repayment

- **Maximum deduction:** Rs. 1,50,000 per year, shared across all 80C investments (PPF, ELSS, LIC, EPF, etc.)
- The principal repayment component of your EMI qualifies
- Stamp duty and registration charges also qualify under 80C — but only in the year of payment

> **Important condition:** if you sell the property within 5 years of possession, all Section 80C deductions claimed will be reversed and added back to your income in the year of sale.

---

## Section 80EEA — First-Time Buyer Extra Benefit

- Applicable for loans sanctioned between **1 April 2019 and 31 March 2022** for affordable housing (stamp duty value not exceeding Rs. 45 lakh)
- Deduction: up to **Rs. 1,50,000 additional interest** — over and above Section 24(b)
- If you are still repaying such a loan in FY 2026–27, you can continue to claim this benefit

---

## Joint Home Loan: The Rs. 7 Lakh Annual Shield

If both co-applicants are also co-owners of the property, each can independently claim the full deductions:

| Deduction | Per Person | Combined (couple) |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Section 24(b) interest | Rs. 2,00,000 | Rs. 4,00,000 |
| Section 80C principal | Rs. 1,50,000 | Rs. 3,00,000 |
| **Total** | **Rs. 3,50,000** | **Rs. 7,00,000** |

This is one of the most powerful tax-saving strategies available in India in 2026.

---

## Old Regime vs New Regime: Which Works for You?

As a rule of thumb: **if your total deductions exceed Rs. 4–5 lakh, the old regime is likely better.**

For a borrower in the 30% tax bracket with Rs. 2 lakh interest + Rs. 1.5 lakh principal:
- Annual tax saving = Rs. 3.5 lakh × 30% = **Rs. 1,05,000**

---

## Pre-Construction Interest: Often Missed

Interest paid during the construction phase (before possession) is NOT deductible in the year paid. After possession, it can be claimed **in 5 equal annual instalments** under Section 24(b), subject to the Rs. 2,00,000 SOP cap.

**Example:** if total pre-construction interest was Rs. 5,00,000 and possession was in June 2025, you can claim Rs. 1,00,000 per year from FY 2025–26 through FY 2029–30.
