## How UC Property Home Loans Work in 2026

For under-construction (UC) properties, the bank does not disburse the full loan amount upfront. Disbursement happens **in tranches linked to construction milestones** — verified by the bank's technical team. As each tranche is disbursed, interest accrues on that amount. This period is called the **Pre-EMI phase**, and it ends when full disbursement happens and possession is given.

---

## Pre-EMI vs Full EMI: The Critical Distinction

| Type | What You Pay | Principal Reduction |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Pre-EMI** | Interest only on disbursed amount | ❌ No |
| **Full EMI** | Principal + Interest | ✅ Yes |

**Choosing between Pre-EMI and Full EMI:** some lenders offer the option to start full EMI from Day 1 even during construction — this reduces the outstanding principal and total interest, but requires higher outgo during construction.

## Typical Construction-Linked Disbursement Schedule — 2026

| Construction Stage | Disbursement % |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Agreement signing + foundation completion | 10%–15% |
| Plinth, basement, ground floor columns | 15%–20% |
| Roof slab and framing up to buyer's floor | 15%–20% |
| Brickwork and external plastering | 10%–15% |
| Flooring, tiles, electrical fittings, plumbing | 10% |
| Occupation Certificate (OC) + possession | 10%–15% (final) |

## Tax Treatment of Pre-EMI Interest

Pre-EMI interest paid during the construction phase **cannot be claimed as a deduction in the year paid**. After possession, the total Pre-EMI interest is aggregated and claimed in **5 equal annual instalments** under Section 24(b), subject to the Rs. 2,00,000 SOP annual cap.

**Example:** total Pre-EMI interest Rs. 4.5 lakh over 3 years of construction. Possession: June 2025.
You can claim Rs. 90,000/year from FY 2025–26 to FY 2029–30.

## RERA Protection for UC Buyers in April 2026

RERA mandates that **70% of all buyer funds** (including loan disbursals) must be deposited into a dedicated escrow account. Withdrawals are permitted only against construction progress certified by an engineer and architect.

This prevents builders from diverting funds to other projects — a major problem that plagued the sector pre-RERA.

---

## Red Flags to Watch for in 2026

1. **Non-RERA project** — avoid entirely; banks will not finance and regulatory protection is absent

2. **Builder requesting payment before construction milestones** — violates RERA; report to state RERA authority

3. **Delayed OC** — Occupation Certificate is required before banks release the final tranche and before you can legally occupy the flat

4. **Deviation from approved plan** — if the bank's technical team finds construction deviating from approved plans, disbursement stops
