## Home Loan vs LAP: The Core Difference

Both are secured loans against property — but they serve entirely different purposes:

- **Home Loan:** for buying, building, or renovating a home
- **Loan Against Property (LAP):** mortgages an existing property to fund any legitimate need

---

## Rate Comparison — April 2026

| Product | Rate Range | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Home Loan | **7.10%–9.50%** p.a. | Floating, EBLR-linked |
| LAP | **9.00%–13.00%** p.a. | 2%–4% higher than home loans |

**Why the gap:** home loans carry lower default risk (borrower lives in the funded property); LAP has no restriction on end-use which creates higher lender risk.

**On Rs. 50 lakh for 15 years:**
- Home loan at 7.5% = total interest **Rs. 36.2 lakh**
- LAP at 10% = total interest **Rs. 49.8 lakh**
- **Difference: Rs. 13.6 lakh**

## LTV Ratio Comparison — April 2026

| Product | LTV Ratio |
|---------|----------|
| Home loan (up to Rs. 30L) | Up to **90%** |
| Home loan (Rs. 30–75L) | Up to **80%** |
| Home loan (above Rs. 75L) | Up to **75%** |
| LAP | **55%–70%** of current market value |

**Implication:** a Rs. 1 crore property can support a home loan of up to Rs. 90 lakh (if eligible) but a LAP of only Rs. 55–70 lakh.

## Tenure Comparison

| Product | Maximum Tenure |
|---------|---------------|
| Home Loan | Up to **30 years** |
| LAP | Up to **15–20 years** |

Shorter LAP tenure = higher EMI for the same loan amount.

---

## When to Choose Home Loan

**Always choose a home loan** if you are buying, constructing, renovating, or extending a residential property. The lower rate, longer tenure, higher LTV, and significant tax benefits make it clearly superior.

## When to Choose LAP

Choose LAP if you need funds for **business expansion, medical emergency, children's education, or any other legitimate non-property purpose** and you have an existing owned property. LAP is significantly cheaper than a personal loan (9%–13% vs 11%–18%).
