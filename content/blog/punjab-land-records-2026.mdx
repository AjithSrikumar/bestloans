## Punjab Land Records in 2026: PLRS and Apna Khata

Punjab's land records are managed by the **Punjab Land Records Society (PLRS)** through the **Apna Khata portal**. In 2026, the portal provides online access to Jamabandi (Record of Rights), Fard (certified extract), mutation status, and cadastral maps for all villages across Punjab.

---

## Key Documents Available on Punjab Apna Khata

| Document | Description |
|----------|------------|
| **Jamabandi** | Primary record of rights — owner name, khewat number, khatauni number, survey/khasra number, area, land use |
| **Fard** | Certified copy/extract from Jamabandi — used as official proof of ownership |
| **Mutation (Intiqal)** | Records transfer of ownership after sale, inheritance, or gift |
| **Shajra Nasb (Map)** | Cadastral map showing plot boundaries, dimensions, and neighbouring plots |

---

## How to Access Punjab Land Records — April 2026

1. Visit **jamabandi.punjab.gov.in** (Apna Khata portal)
2. Select district, tehsil, and village
3. Choose the record type (Jamabandi, Mutation, Girdawari)
4. Enter khewat number, khatauni number, or owner name
5. View and download the record
6. For **certified Fard**: visit the nearest Patwar Khana or apply online through the portal

---

## Mutation Process in Punjab — 2026

In Punjab, mutation is applied for at the Revenue Patwari's office after registering the property.

**Process:**
1. Registration of sale deed at sub-registrar office
2. Apply for mutation at Patwar Khana with sale deed, ID proof, and photographs
3. Mutation entry made in Jamabandi after verification by Kanungo and Tehsildar
4. **Timeline:** 30–45 days typically

## Using Punjab Land Records for Home Loan Applications

Banks and HFCs lending against properties in Punjab require:

| Document | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Latest Jamabandi | Not more than 6 months old |
| Encumbrance Certificate (Dosal Malkaan) | Confirming no existing mortgage |
| Mutation completion | Updated Jamabandi in buyer's name before final disbursement |

> **Obtain these documents before applying for a home loan in Punjab** — missing or outdated records are a common cause of disbursement delays.
