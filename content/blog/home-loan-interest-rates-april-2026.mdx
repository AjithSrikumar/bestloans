## April 2026: A Borrower-Friendly Rate Environment

Following 125 basis points of cumulative RBI rate cuts through 2025, and with the repo rate steady at 5.25% in April 2026, home loan interest rates are at multi-year lows. The lowest available rate in the market is **7.10% p.a.** — versus 9%+ in the 2022–23 rate-hike cycle.

This 2% differential translates to over **Rs. 7 lakh saved** on a Rs. 50 lakh, 20-year loan.

## Fixed vs Floating Rate — April 2026 Verdict

Fixed rates currently start at around **9.50%+ p.a.** — significantly higher than floating. With the RBI in a neutral stance and rates stable, **floating is clearly the better choice** for most borrowers. If rates drop further in FY2026–27, floating borrowers automatically benefit.

## 5 Proven Tips to Get the Lowest Rate in April 2026

1. **CIBIL 750+:** lenders assign their best rate slabs to borrowers above 750. A score of 800+ can get you rates 0.15%–0.25% below what a 720 score gets

2. **Women co-applicant:** nearly all lenders offer a 0.05% concession when a woman is the primary or first co-applicant — that is a Rs. 30,000+ saving on a Rs. 40 lakh loan over 20 years

3. **Government or top-tier employer:** SBI, Bank of Baroda, and PNB offer preferential rates for government employees

4. **Negotiate the spread:** EBLR = repo + spread. The spread is negotiable — aim for 2.00%–2.50% spread over 5.25% repo

5. **Balance transfer from older loans:** if you have a loan at 8.5%+, a balance transfer to 7.5% can save Rs. 5–8 lakh on Rs. 40 lakh outstanding over 15 years
