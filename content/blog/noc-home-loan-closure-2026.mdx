## Why NOC is Non-Negotiable After Loan Closure

A No Objection Certificate (NOC) — also called No Dues Certificate or Loan Closure Letter — is the lender's formal confirmation that the home loan is fully repaid and the lender has no further claim on your property.

Without it, the **mortgage lien remains active in CERSAI** (Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India) records, making future sale, refinance, or property transfer extremely difficult.

---

## RBI's 30-Day Document Return Rule — 2026

As per the Reserve Bank of India's updated guidelines (effective 2023 and in full force in 2026):

> **Lenders must return all original property documents within 30 days of full loan repayment.**

If delayed beyond 30 days, the borrower is entitled to a **compensation of Rs. 5,000 per day** for the delay. This is a legally enforceable right — escalate to the Banking Ombudsman if not complied with.

## Complete NOC Process — Step by Step

**Step 1:** Make final EMI payment; confirm outstanding balance via loan statement or bank app

**Step 2:** Request a Loan Closure Statement — official confirmation of zero outstanding

**Step 3:** Submit written NOC request (branch visit / mobile banking app / lender portal — most banks now process digitally)

**Step 4:** Receive NOC within 7–30 days; also receive all original property documents held as security

**Step 5:** Review the NOC carefully — verify loan account number, property address, borrower name, closure date

---

## Documents You Must Collect with the NOC

- Original property title deed and chain documents
- Original sale agreement / conveyance deed
- Original possession letter from builder
- Building plan approval copy
- Any other originals submitted during loan processing

---

## Post-NOC Actions — April 2026 Checklist

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| CERSAI removal | File a request to remove the mortgage from CERSAI records |
| Sub-registrar update | Visit local sub-registrar to remove mortgage/lien entry from property records |
| CIBIL update | Loan account should show 'Closed' within 30–45 days; monitor your credit report |
| Encumbrance Certificate | Obtain a fresh EC 2–3 months after closure to confirm property is now lien-free |
| Home insurance | If you had lender-mandated home insurance, check if the policy needs reassignment |
