## PMAY-G in April 2026: What is Still Active

PMAY-Gramin (PMAY-G) — the rural housing scheme under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana — continues to be active in April 2026 under **Phase III**. It targets rural households living in kutcha (temporary) or inadequate shelters to build permanent pucca homes with basic amenities. Financial assistance is transferred directly to beneficiaries' **Aadhaar-linked bank accounts** via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

---

## PMAY-G Financial Assistance — April 2026

| Category | Assistance Amount |
|----------|------------------|
| **Plain areas** | Rs. 1,20,000 per household for new pucca house construction |
| **Hilly/difficult terrain areas** | Rs. 1,30,000 per household |
| **Minimum house size** | 25 square metres (increased from earlier 20 sq. metres) |
| **Toilet provision** | Rs. 12,000 (linked with Swachh Bharat Mission-Gramin) |
| **Cooking fuel** | LPG connection linked with Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana |
| **Electricity** | Linked with Saubhagya Yojana for household electrification |

## PMAY-G Eligibility — April 2026

- Household must be listed in **SECC (Socio-Economic and Caste Census) 2011** as lacking a proper house
- Priority given to: households with no adult member aged 16–59; female-headed households; SC/ST families; minorities; persons with disabilities; bonded labourers
- Household must **not already own a pucca house** anywhere in India
- Must **not have previously benefited** from any state or central housing scheme

## How to Check PMAY-G Beneficiary List — April 2026

1. Visit **awaassoft.nic.in** → 'Beneficiary Status' → select your state, district, block, panchayat
2. Visit **rhreporting.nic.in** for detailed district-wise beneficiary reports
3. Search by your **Aadhaar number or Registration Number** at the Awaassoft portal
4. Visit your **Gram Panchayat office** — beneficiary lists are displayed publicly

---

## How to Apply for PMAY-G in 2026

**Existing SECC 2011-listed households:** Selection is automatic through the SECC data. If your name is in the SECC list but not yet selected as a beneficiary, approach your **Gram Panchayat or Block Development Officer (BDO)** with your SECC registration details.

**New applicants not in SECC:** Approach the **District Rural Development Agency (DRDA)** or Gram Panchayat for any state-specific waiting lists or appeals process.

---

## PMAY-G and Home Loans: Is a Bank Loan Required?

PMAY-G financial assistance is a **direct grant — not a loan.** It does not require you to take a home loan. However, if the assistance amount is insufficient for construction, beneficiaries can supplement with a home loan from banks or microfinance institutions. SBI and other public sector banks have specific lending schemes for PMAY-G beneficiaries at concessional rates.
