## Why Your CIBIL Score Matters for a Home Loan

Your CIBIL score (credit score) is the single most important factor banks consider when approving your home loan. It directly impacts:

- **Loan approval/rejection**
- **Interest rate offered** (better score = lower rate)
- **Loan amount eligibility**
- **Processing speed**

A score of 750+ can save you 0.5–1.5% on your interest rate — that's ₹5–15 lakhs in savings on a ₹50 lakh loan over 20 years.

## What is a Good CIBIL Score?

| Score Range | Rating | Home Loan Impact |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| 750–900 | Excellent | Best rates, instant approval |
| 700–749 | Good | Good rates, smooth approval |
| 650–699 | Fair | Higher rates, careful scrutiny |
| 600–649 | Poor | Difficult to get loan |
| Below 600 | Very Poor | Loan rejection likely |

## 10 Proven Ways to Improve Your CIBIL Score

### 1. Pay All EMIs and Credit Card Bills on Time

**Impact: Very High | Timeline: 1–6 months**

Payment history accounts for 35% of your CIBIL score. Even one missed payment can drop your score by 50–100 points.

**Tips:**
- Set up auto-pay for all EMIs
- Pay credit card bills before the due date (not just minimum amount)
- Use calendar reminders as backup

### 2. Reduce Credit Card Utilization

**Impact: High | Timeline: 1–2 months**

Keep your credit card usage below 30% of your credit limit. If your limit is ₹1 lakh, don't use more than ₹30,000.

**Example:** If you have a ₹1 lakh card and use ₹80,000 regularly:
- Current utilization: 80% (BAD)
- Target utilization: Below 30% (GOOD)

**Tips:**
- Request a credit limit increase without increasing spending
- Pay bills mid-cycle (before statement generation)
- Use multiple cards to distribute spending

### 3. Don't Close Old Credit Cards

Old credit accounts contribute positively to your credit history length. Closing them can reduce your average account age and hurt your score.

**Exception:** Close if there's an annual fee and you're not using it.

### 4. Avoid Multiple Loan Applications

Every time you apply for a loan or credit card, the lender does a "hard inquiry" that temporarily reduces your score by 5–10 points.

**Tip:** Use BestLoans — we check your eligibility across 25+ banks with a single soft inquiry.

### 5. Resolve Errors in Your Credit Report

Up to 30% of credit reports contain errors. A wrong default or missed payment that wasn't yours can unfairly hurt your score.

**How to dispute:**
1. Download your detailed CIBIL report
2. Identify incorrect entries
3. File a dispute on the CIBIL portal
4. Follow up every 30 days

Resolution typically takes 30–45 days.

### 6. Maintain a Healthy Credit Mix

Having both secured loans (home loan, car loan) and unsecured credit (credit cards) shows responsible borrowing.

**Ideal mix:** 1–2 credit cards + 1–2 loans

### 7. Become an Authorized User

Ask a family member with excellent credit to add you as an authorized user on their credit card. Their positive history benefits your score.

### 8. Settle (Don't Just Close) Defaulted Accounts

If you have defaulted accounts, negotiate a settlement with the lender and ensure they report it as "settled" or "closed." A settled account is better than an open default.

### 9. Don't Apply for Credit You Don't Need

Resist the temptation of pre-approved loans and credit cards. Every hard inquiry reduces your score.

### 10. Be Patient and Consistent

Credit improvement is a marathon, not a sprint. With consistent effort:
- **3 months:** Small improvements (10–30 points)
- **6 months:** Significant improvement (30–80 points)
- **12 months:** Major transformation possible

## Month-by-Month Action Plan

**Month 1:**
- Check your CIBIL report (identify errors)
- Set up auto-pay for all bills
- Calculate credit utilization and reduce if needed

**Month 2–3:**
- File disputes for any errors found
- Keep utilization below 30%
- Don't apply for any new credit

**Month 4–6:**
- Monitor score monthly
- Continue on-time payments
- Consider requesting credit limit increases

## When to Apply for a Home Loan

- Score 750+: Apply now — you'll get the best rates
- Score 700–749: Apply now, but work on improvement in parallel
- Score 650–699: Wait 3–6 months and improve first
- Score below 650: Wait 6–12 months and work aggressively on improvement
