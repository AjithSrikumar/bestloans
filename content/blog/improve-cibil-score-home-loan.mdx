## Why Your CIBIL Score Matters for a Home Loan

Your CIBIL score (credit score) is the single most important factor banks evaluate when approving your home loan application. It directly impacts:

- **Loan approval** — scores below 650 often lead to rejection
- **Interest rate** — a 750+ score can get you 0.5–1.5% lower rates
- **Loan amount** — higher scores unlock higher eligibility
- **Processing speed** — good credit profiles get faster approvals

A score of **750+** can save you ₹5–15 lakhs on a ₹50 lakh loan over 20 years. That's worth every effort.

## What is a Good CIBIL Score?

| Score Range | Rating | Home Loan Impact |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| 750 – 900 | Excellent | Best rates, instant approval |
| 700 – 749 | Good | Good rates, smooth approval |
| 650 – 699 | Fair | Higher rates, closer scrutiny |
| 600 – 649 | Poor | Difficult to get approved |
| Below 600 | Very Poor | Loan rejection likely |

## 8 Proven Ways to Improve Your CIBIL Score

### 1. Pay All EMIs and Credit Card Bills on Time

**Impact: Very High | Timeline: 1–6 months**

Payment history accounts for **35% of your CIBIL score**. Even one missed payment can drop your score by 50–100 points instantly.

- Set up auto-pay for all EMIs
- Pay credit card bills in full before the due date (not just the minimum)
- Use calendar reminders as a backup

### 2. Reduce Your Credit Card Utilization

**Impact: High | Timeline: 1–2 months**

Keep your credit card usage below **30% of your credit limit** at all times.

| Card Limit | Safe Usage (30%) | Your Target |
|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| ₹1,00,000 | ₹30,000 | Pay off before statement date |
| ₹50,000 | ₹15,000 | Never exceed this |
| ₹2,00,000 | ₹60,000 | Ideal ceiling |

*Tips: Request a credit limit increase without spending more. Pay mid-cycle to lower statement balance.*

### 3. Don't Close Old Credit Cards

Older credit accounts increase your **average credit age**, which positively impacts your score. Closing them can reduce your score by 20–40 points.

**Exception:** Close only if the card has a high annual fee and you're not using it at all.

### 4. Avoid Multiple Loan Applications

Every loan or credit card application triggers a **hard inquiry** that temporarily reduces your score by 5–10 points. Multiple applications within a short period signal financial stress to banks.

**Better approach:** Use BestLoans — we check your eligibility across 25+ banks with a single soft inquiry.

### 5. Dispute Errors in Your Credit Report

Up to **30% of credit reports contain errors** — a missed payment that wasn't yours, a wrong account, or an outdated settlement can unfairly drag your score down.

- Download your full CIBIL report
- Identify incorrect entries
- Raise a dispute on the CIBIL website
- Follow up every 30 days until resolved (typically 30–45 days)

### 6. Maintain a Healthy Mix of Credit

Having both **secured credit** (home loan, car loan) and **unsecured credit** (credit cards) shows disciplined borrowing. A diverse credit portfolio improves your score over time.

### 7. Settle (Don't Ignore) Defaulted Accounts

If you have old defaults, negotiate a settlement with the lender and ensure they report it as **"Settled"** or **"Closed"** — not "Written Off." Even a settled account is significantly better than an open default.

### 8. Be Patient — Credit Improvement Takes Time

| Timeline | Expected Improvement |
|----------|---------------------|
| 1 month | Small changes, errors resolved |
| 3 months | 10–30 point improvement |
| 6 months | 30–80 point improvement |
| 12 months | Major transformation possible |

## Your 6-Month Action Plan

**Month 1–2:**
- Check your CIBIL report and dispute any errors
- Set up auto-pay for all bills
- Reduce credit card utilization below 30%

**Month 3–4:**
- Don't apply for any new credit
- Keep making on-time payments consistently
- Monitor your score monthly (soft inquiry only)

**Month 5–6:**
- Consider requesting a credit limit increase
- Review if old accounts should be reactivated
- Apply for your home loan once score hits 720+
