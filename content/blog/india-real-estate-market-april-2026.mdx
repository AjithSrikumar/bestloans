## India Real Estate: Where We Stand in April 2026

India's residential real estate market entered FY2026–27 from a position of quiet strength. Housing sales across top 7 cities held firm, with the total market value surpassing **Rs. 6.65 lakh crore in FY2026** — a near 20% year-on-year increase in value despite a modest 3% volume decline.

The defining shift: fewer units sold at significantly higher prices — **average ticket size reaching Rs. 1.47 crore**.

---

## Q1 2026 Data: Six-Metro Snapshot (Jan–Mar 2026)

India's six major metros launched **1,04,882 residential units** across 633 RERA-registered projects in Q1 2026. Performance was highly uneven across cities:

| City | Trend | Key Drivers |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Bengaluru | **Strongest market** in Q1 2026 | Namma Metro Phase 3 expansion driving peripheral demand |
| NCR (Delhi/Gurugram/Noida) | Steady premium demand | Golf Course Road, Dwarka Expressway, Noida Expressway corridors |
| Mumbai/MMR | Premium segment firm | Navi Mumbai gaining from new international airport |
| Chennai | **Surging** launch activity | Manufacturing sector growth; mid-income outperforming |
| Pune | End-user driven | Wakad, Hinjewadi, Kharadi suburban markets outperforming |
| Hyderabad | **Sharpest decline** | Launches fell 46% YoY to 9,700 units in Q1 2026 |

## Key Trends: What is Driving the Market in 2026

**Premiumisation:** homes priced above Rs. 1 crore accounted for **62% of total sales** (JLL). Affordable housing supply has shrunk as rising land and construction costs squeeze margins.

**Private equity surge:** real estate PE inflows reached **$6.7 billion in 2025** — a 59% year-on-year record. $1.2 billion flowing in Q1 2026 alone.

**Tier 2 expansion:** Jaipur, Lucknow, Kochi, Indore, Bhopal, Nashik seeing **10–15% price appreciation** annually; major developers expanding into these markets.

**Infrastructure-led growth:** new expressways, metro corridors, and the Navi Mumbai airport are creating new residential pockets in previously underserved locations.

---

## Price Outlook: 2026–2027

A Reuters poll of property analysts (February–March 2026) forecasts home prices in major urban centres to rise **5%–7% annually** over the next 3 years. Tier 2 cities are forecast to outperform at **10%–15%**.

With home loan rates at 7.10%–7.25% and property prices rising, the affordability window of 2026 is real — but not permanent.

## Buyer Strategy for April 2026

1. **RERA-registered projects only** — banks will not finance non-RERA under-construction property

2. **Bengaluru, NCR, and Chennai** offer the best combination of supply, infrastructure growth, and rental demand

3. **In Hyderabad:** negotiate hard — the 46% launch decline means unsold inventory is available at competitive prices

4. **Tier 2 cities:** Jaipur, Lucknow, and Kochi offer 60%–70% lower prices than metros with 10–15% annual appreciation potential
