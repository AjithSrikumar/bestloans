## Stamp Duty: The Hidden Cost That Changes Everything

Stamp duty and registration charges are mandatory costs that buyers often underestimate. They add **4%–11% to the total property cost** depending on the state, and unlike most other costs, they **cannot be financed through the home loan** — they must be paid from personal funds.

Understanding these charges before signing any agreement is essential for accurate budgeting.

## Concessions Available in April 2026

- **Women buyers:** 1%–2% stamp duty concession across most states
- **PMAY-linked properties:** some states offer stamp duty waivers or reductions for PMAY beneficiaries
- **First-time buyers:** some states offer temporary concessions during fiscal year-end periods
- **Agricultural land/commercial property:** different rates apply — always verify with the state's registration department

## How Stamp Duty Affects Your Home Loan Budget

Stamp duty and registration are **NOT covered by the home loan**. Plan for **6%–8% of property value** as additional costs over your down payment.

**Example:** For a Rs. 80 lakh property in Mumbai (male buyer), effective stamp duty + registration ≈ 7% = **Rs. 5.6 lakh** — paid upfront.

**The silver lining:** stamp duty paid in the year of purchase qualifies for deduction under **Section 80C** of the Income Tax Act (within the Rs. 1.5 lakh overall limit).
