## What is a Home Loan Processing Fee?

A home loan processing fee is a one-time charge collected by the lender to cover the cost of evaluating your loan application — credit assessment, property legal verification, technical valuation, and administrative processing. It is typically charged as a percentage of the sanctioned loan amount and is generally **non-refundable** once the application has been processed, even if the loan is not taken.

## Other Charges Beyond Processing Fee

| Charge | Typical Range |
|--------|--------------|
| Legal/advocate fee | Rs. 5,000–20,000 depending on property location and title complexity |
| Technical valuation fee | Rs. 3,000–8,000 for bank's empanelled engineer/valuer |
| MODT stamp duty | Varies by state; 0.1%–0.5% of loan amount |
| Loan agreement stamp duty | Rs. 200–500 depending on state |
| GST on all fee components | 18% |

**Plan for approximately 1.5%–3% of loan amount** in total fees beyond the down payment.

## How to Get the Processing Fee Waived in 2026

**Festive offers:** Most banks waive processing fees during Navratri, Dussehra, and Diwali — the biggest fee-waiver windows of the year.

**Year-end push:** Banks with targets to meet in March often waive fees to close more disbursements.

**Negotiate directly:** If you have a CIBIL score above 750, a large loan amount (Rs. 50 lakh+), or a salary account with the bank, you have strong negotiating leverage — ask directly for a waiver.

**Salary account holder:** Most banks offer processing fee waivers to customers who have an active salary account with them.

## Is the Processing Fee Refundable If Loan is Not Disbursed?

Technically it is **non-refundable**. However, if the loan is not sanctioned for reasons within the bank's control (e.g., internal process delay), some lenders refund a portion. If the loan is sanctioned but you withdraw your application, most banks retain the entire fee.

Always **confirm the refund policy in writing** before paying the application/processing fee.
