## Why 2026 is a Great Year for Women to Buy a Home

India's housing finance sector in 2026 actively incentivises women homeownership through multiple overlapping benefits: lower interest rates, stamp duty concessions, PMAY preferential eligibility, better approval rates, and stronger legal protections.

With home loan rates starting at **7.10% p.a.** and the real estate market offering strong value in tier 2 cities, the conditions are particularly favourable.

---

## Interest Rate Concession for Women Borrowers

Most major lenders in April 2026 offer women borrowers a **0.05%–0.10% interest rate reduction** compared to standard rates, subject to the woman being the primary borrower or first co-applicant.

**Banks offering this concession:**
- SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank
- Bank of Baroda, PNB
- Kotak Mahindra Bank
- Union Bank of India, Canara Bank

Over a 20-year Rs. 50 lakh loan, a 0.05% concession saves approximately **Rs. 30,000** in total interest.

## PMAY Priority for Women

Under **PMAY-U 2.0**, women must be either the sole owner or a co-owner of the property for EWS and LIG category beneficiaries to receive the interest subsidy. The scheme also gives **priority in allotment** to women-headed households, widows, and single women.

This makes female co-ownership practically mandatory to maximise PMAY benefits.

---

## Better Approval Odds and Higher LTV

Data analytics from multiple lenders shows **lower home loan default rates among women borrowers**. As a result:
- Banks are more likely to approve applications with women co-applicants
- Some lenders offer a preferential LTV ratio of up to **85%** (vs 80% for male-only applications) for the same loan amount bracket

---

## Tax Benefits — Same Plus Joint Ownership Doubles Them

Women borrowers are entitled to the same tax benefits as any other borrower:
- Up to **Rs. 2,00,000** under Section 24(b) — interest deduction
- Up to **Rs. 1,50,000** under Section 80C — principal repayment

When a woman jointly owns the property with a spouse, both can independently claim these limits, creating a household deduction shield of **Rs. 7,00,000 annually**.
