## Self-Employed Home Loans in 2026: The Landscape

Over 40% of India's working population is self-employed, and in 2026, banks have refined their underwriting for this segment. The focus has shifted from simple ITR verification to **holistic income assessment** — bank turnover analysis, GST filing trends, and business vintage.

The golden rule: **demonstrate stable, growing, tax-paid income across at least 3 years.**

---

## Two Categories: SEP vs SENP

| Category | Who | Lender View |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| **SEP** (Self-Employed Professionals) | Doctors, CAs, lawyers, architects, engineers, consultants | More favourable — closer to salaried underwriting |
| **SENP** (Self-Employed Non-Professionals) | Traders, manufacturers, shopkeepers, contractors, small business owners | Variable income; surrogate methods often used |

---

## Eligibility Norms — April 2026

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Business continuity | **3 years** (most banks); 2 years at select NBFCs |
| Minimum age | 23 years at application |
| Maximum age | 70 years at loan maturity |
| Minimum net annual profit | Rs. 2 lakh+ post-tax |
| CIBIL score | 700+ (750+ for best rates) |
| FOIR | Total monthly obligations ≤ 50–55% of net monthly income |

---

## Documentation — April 2026 Requirements

| Document | Details |
|----------|---------|
| ITR with income computation | Last 3 financial years (e-filed, acknowledged) |
| CA-certified Balance Sheet + P&L | Last 3 years (audited for larger businesses) |
| Business continuity proof | GST registration + returns (last 2 years), Trade Licence, or Partnership Deed |
| Business bank account statements | Last 12 months (primary operating account) |
| Tax Audit Report | If turnover exceeds Rs. 1 crore (business) or receipts exceed Rs. 25 lakh (professionals) |
| GST returns (GSTR-3B) | Last 12 months — increasingly used as income surrogate |

---

## The Biggest Challenge: Low Declared Income on ITR

Many self-employed borrowers understate income on ITR to reduce tax liability — then find their home loan eligibility severely constrained.

In 2026, there are two paths:
- **(a) File accurate ITRs for 2–3 years** before applying — takes time but gives the best terms
- **(b) Use bank turnover / GST surrogate method** with lenders who accept it — faster results but often higher rates

---

## Tips for Self-Employed Applicants in April 2026

1. **Route all business income through a bank account** — large cash transactions invisible to lenders are invisible to lenders
2. **Ensure your GST filings are current** — lenders are increasingly using GSTN data as an income verification tool
3. **Add a salaried co-applicant** (spouse or parent) if business income documentation is weak
4. **Compare NBFCs** like LIC HFL, PNB Housing, Bajaj Housing, and IIFL — they often have more nuanced self-employed underwriting
