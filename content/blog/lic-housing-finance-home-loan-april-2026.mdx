## LIC Housing Finance — April 2026 Overview

LIC Housing Finance Limited (LIC HFL) is India's second-largest housing finance company and one of the most trusted non-bank home loan providers. As a subsidiary of LIC of India, it carries enormous brand trust — particularly with salaried borrowers and those planning long-tenure loans. In April 2026, LIC HFL rates start at **7.50% p.a.** for CIBIL 750+ salaried borrowers — competitive in the HFC space.

| Borrower Type | Interest Rate |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Salaried (CIBIL 750+)** | From 7.50% p.a. (floating, PLR-linked) |
| **Salaried (CIBIL 700–749)** | Approximately 7.75%–8.25% p.a. |
| **Self-employed** | From 7.80% p.a. |

- **Processing fee:** 0.25%–0.50% of loan amount + GST — known for lower processing fees than private banks

## LIC HFL Home Loan Schemes — April 2026

| Scheme | Best For |
|--------|---------|
| **Griha Suvidha** | Flagship product for purchase, construction, renovation. Up to 30 years, LTV up to 90% for loans ≤ Rs. 30 lakh |
| **Griha Varishtha** | Specially designed for senior citizens (age 60+). Pension income considered for eligibility |
| **Griha Pragati** | Under-construction properties with construction-linked disbursement and competitive pre-EMI rates |
| **Griha Shakthi** | Specifically for women borrowers at concessional rates with lower processing fees |
| **Griha Utsav** | Special festive offer launched periodically with processing fee waivers and rate concessions |

## LIC HFL Eligibility — April 2026

- **Age:** 21–60 years (salaried); 21–65 years (self-employed)
- **CIBIL score:** 700+ for standard approval; 750+ for best rates
- **Employment:** Salaried — minimum 2 years work experience; self-employed — **5 years business vintage** (slightly stricter than banks)
- **Unique advantage:** LIC HFL is one of the few lenders that **accepts LIC policy surrender value and bonus as income** for loan eligibility enhancement

---

## LIC HFL vs Bank Home Loans — When to Choose LIC HFL

**Choose LIC HFL if:**
- You are a long-tenure salaried borrower wanting an HFC rather than a bank
- You are a senior citizen (Griha Varishtha scheme)
- You have LIC policies and want to leverage them for eligibility

**Choose a bank if:**
- You want the absolute lowest rate (SBI at 7.25% beats LIC HFL's 7.50%)
- You want the fastest processing (HDFC, ICICI are typically faster)
