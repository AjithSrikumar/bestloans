## Kotak Mahindra Bank Home Loan — April 2026 Overview

Kotak Mahindra Bank is one of India's premier private sector banks with a strong home loan portfolio. In April 2026, Kotak home loan rates start at **7.99% p.a.** for salaried borrowers with CIBIL 750+ — making it competitive among private banks though slightly above SBI and HDFC's best slabs.

Kotak is particularly strong on **digital processing, NRI home loans, and premium property financing.**

---

## Kotak Home Loan Interest Rates — April 2026

| Borrower Type | Interest Rate |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Salaried (CIBIL 750+)** | From 7.99% p.a. (floating) |
| **Salaried (CIBIL 700–749)** | Approximately 8.25%–8.75% p.a. |
| **Self-employed professionals** | From 8.15% p.a. |
| **Women borrowers (CIBIL 750+)** | From 7.94% p.a. (additional 0.05% concession) |

- **Processing fee:** Up to 0.50% of loan amount + GST. Waivers available for salary account holders and during festive campaigns.

---

## Kotak Home Loan Eligibility — April 2026

- **Age:** 18–65 years (loan must close before 65 for salaried; 70 for self-employed)
- **CIBIL score:** 750+ for best rates; 700–749 approved at higher rates
- **Employment:** Salaried — minimum 2 years experience, 6+ months at current employer
- **Self-employed:** 3+ years business continuity with consistent ITR filing
- **Loan amount:** Rs. 20 lakh to Rs. 10 crore for premium properties in metro cities

---

## Kotak Home Loan for NRI Borrowers

Kotak offers a well-structured **NRI Home Loan** product with:
- Rates comparable to resident Indian rates
- Digital application from abroad
- Acceptance of foreign bank statements
- NRE/NRO accounts accepted for repayment
- Largely paperless application process

---

## Kotak vs HDFC vs ICICI — Positioning in April 2026

HDFC Bank offers slightly lower starting rates (7.20%) vs Kotak (7.99%). However, Kotak is preferred by certain HNI borrowers for its **personalised service, relationship banking model, and willingness to process premium property transactions** (above Rs. 5 crore) that some other banks are slower to handle.

For standard Rs. 30–50 lakh loans, HDFC or SBI typically offer better value.
