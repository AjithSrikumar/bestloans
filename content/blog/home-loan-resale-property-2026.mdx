## Home Loan for Resale Property vs New Property — Key Differences

Resale properties (also called secondary market or pre-owned flats/houses) involve buying a property from an existing owner rather than a builder. The home loan process differs from new property purchases in important ways:

- Additional title history checks (13–30 years)
- Property age assessment
- Society NOC requirements
- More complex document chains

---

## What Banks Verify for Resale Properties — April 2026

| Verification | What Banks Check |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Title chain** | Every transfer of ownership must be documented — any missing link causes rejection |
| **Encumbrance Certificate** | Must confirm zero existing mortgages or legal disputes for the past 13–30 years (varies by lender) |
| **Property age** | Most banks have an upper limit — typically 30–40 years old at purchase. Older properties may require a structural inspection |
| **Occupancy Certificate** | Confirms the building was legally constructed per approved plans |
| **Society NOC** | Housing society must confirm the seller has no outstanding dues |
| **Share certificate** | For co-operative societies, must be transferred from seller to buyer |
| **Property tax receipts** | Last 3–5 years' receipts confirming no arrears |

---

## Documents Required for Resale Home Loan in 2026

**From the seller:**
- Original sale deed (current and all historical link documents)
- Occupancy Certificate (OC)
- Approved building plan
- Encumbrance Certificate (13–30 years)
- Property tax receipts
- Society NOC
- Share certificate

**From the buyer (standard KYC + income documents, plus):**
- Signed Agreement to Sell
- Own contribution payment evidence (own cheque / own bank transfer — not cash)

**Additional checks:**
- Utility bills showing no unpaid dues
- If seller had a home loan, their bank's NOC and release letter confirming loan closed

## Common Issues in Resale Home Loans — and How to Avoid Them

**Missing OC:** Many older buildings in India lack an Occupancy Certificate — banks typically cannot fund such properties. **Verify OC availability before paying any advance.**

**Property age beyond lender limit:** Check the year of construction in the approved plan. Banks compute the remaining useful life — if less than the loan tenure, they may reject.

**Seller's undischarged loan:** If the seller has not fully repaid their home loan on the property, the title is still encumbered. Ensure the seller provides a foreclosure NOC from their lender.

**Illegal alterations:** If the seller has made structural changes not in the approved plan (encroachment, additional floor), banks' technical teams will flag it.
