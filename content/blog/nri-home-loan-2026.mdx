## NRI Home Loan in April 2026: Overview

Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) can purchase residential property in India using home loans from Indian banks and HFCs under RBI's FEMA guidelines. Most major lenders offer **fully digital NRI home loan applications** in 2026 — with video KYC, digital document upload, and online disbursement coordination.

---

## Who Qualifies as an NRI for Home Loan Purposes?

- **Indian passport holders** residing abroad for work, education, or other purposes
- **OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) card holders** — allowed to buy residential and commercial property
- **PIO (Person of Indian Origin) card holders** — similar to OCI in most banking contexts
- NRI does NOT include Persons of Pakistani or Bangladeshi origin — FEMA restricts such purchases

---

## NRI Home Loan Eligibility — April 2026

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Minimum age | 21 years at application |
| Maximum age | 60 years at loan maturity (65 at some lenders) |
| Work experience | Minimum 2 years total; at least 1 year with current foreign employer |
| Income threshold | Higher for UK, USA, UAE vs other regions |
| Indian credit history | NRE/NRO account history and any India-based loans considered |

## Interest Rates for NRI Home Loans — April 2026

NRI home loan rates are essentially the same as resident Indian rates — since April 2026 rates start from **7.25% p.a. (SBI)** and are EBLR-linked. NRI borrowers with strong income profiles and high CIBIL scores can access the same competitive rates.

Some lenders may apply a small premium of **0.05%–0.25%** for NRI loans.

## Documents for NRI Home Loan — April 2026 Checklist

| Category | Documents |
|----------|----------|
| KYC | Valid Indian passport + current residence visa + OCI/PIO card |
| Income | Salary certificate (in English) + salary slips last 3–6 months |
| Bank statements | Foreign bank statements last 6 months + NRE/NRO account last 6 months |
| Employment | Current contract + appointment letter from employer |
| Tax documents | Overseas ITR / tax return from country of residence |
| PoA | Registered and apostilled Power of Attorney (if not physically present in India) |
| Property | Same as resident Indian (title deed, sale agreement, builder NOC, RERA registration) |

## Key FEMA Rules for NRI Property Purchase in 2026

| Property Type | Status |
|--------------|--------|
| Residential property | ✅ NRIs can purchase without RBI permission |
| Commercial property | ✅ Allowed under FEMA guidelines |
| Agricultural land | ❌ Cannot purchase without specific RBI approval |
| Plantation property | ❌ Cannot purchase without specific RBI approval |

**Repatriation of sale proceeds:** allowed up to original foreign exchange invested, subject to FEMA conditions.

**Rental income from Indian property:** taxable in India at **30% TDS** for NRI landlords — account for this in your ROI calculation.
