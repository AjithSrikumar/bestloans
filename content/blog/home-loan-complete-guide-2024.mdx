## What is a Home Loan?

A home loan (also called a mortgage) is a secured loan provided by banks and NBFCs to help you purchase, construct, renovate, or extend a residential property. The property itself serves as collateral until you fully repay the loan.

The bank funds 75–90% of the property value, and you pay the rest as a down payment. You repay the loan in monthly instalments (EMIs) over a tenure of up to 30 years.

---

## Types of Home Loans in India

### Home Purchase Loan

The most common type — used to buy a ready-to-move or under-construction property from a builder or existing owner.

### Home Construction Loan

For building a house on a plot you already own. Funds are disbursed in tranches as construction progresses — not all at once.

### Home Renovation Loan

For repair, renovation, or improvement of an existing property. Typically smaller amounts with shorter tenures.

### Balance Transfer Loan

Transfer your existing home loan to a new lender offering a lower interest rate. Can save lakhs in interest over the remaining tenure.

### Top-up Loan

An additional loan over your existing home loan balance, usable for personal needs. Rates are better than personal loans.

## Home Loan Eligibility: What Banks Look For

Your eligibility is determined by five key factors:

- **Age** — 21 to 65 years (at the time of loan maturity)
- **Income** — Minimum ₹25,000/month for salaried; ₹3 lakh/year for self-employed
- **Credit Score** — 750+ preferred; below 650 makes approval difficult
- **Employment** — Stable job history of 2+ years with the current employer
- **LTV Ratio** — Banks finance up to 90% of the property value

## Documents Required

### For Salaried Employees

- PAN Card + Aadhaar Card
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 6 months' bank statements
- Form 16 / IT Returns (2 years)
- Current employment letter or offer letter

### For Self-Employed / Business Owners

- PAN Card + Aadhaar Card
- Business registration proof (GST, Udyam, etc.)
- IT Returns for last 2–3 years
- Balance sheet + Profit & Loss statement
- Last 12 months' bank statements

## How to Increase Your Loan Eligibility

- Repay all existing EMIs and credit card debt before applying
- Avoid applying for multiple loans simultaneously (each inquiry hurts your CIBIL score)
- Increase your down payment — a higher equity stake gets you better rates
- Show all income sources (rental income, freelance, etc.) to the bank
