## What is a Home Loan?

A home loan (also called a mortgage) is a secured loan provided by banks and NBFCs to help you purchase, construct, renovate, or extend a residential property. The property itself serves as collateral until you fully repay the loan.

## Types of Home Loans in India

### 1. Home Purchase Loan
The most common type – used to buy a ready-to-move or under-construction property.

### 2. Home Construction Loan
For building a house on a plot you already own. Disbursed in tranches based on construction stages.

### 3. Home Renovation Loan
For renovation, repair, or improvement of an existing property.

### 4. Balance Transfer Loan
Transfer your existing home loan to a new lender offering a lower interest rate.

### 5. Top-up Loan
An additional loan over your existing home loan for personal needs.

## Home Loan Eligibility

Your eligibility is determined by:

- **Age**: 21–65 years (at loan maturity)
- **Income**: Minimum ₹25,000/month for salaried; ₹3 lakh/year for self-employed
- **Credit Score**: 700+ preferred (CIBIL score)
- **Employment**: Stable job history of 2+ years
- **LTV Ratio**: Banks finance up to 90% of property value

## Documents Required

### For Salaried Employees:
- PAN Card & Aadhaar Card
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 6 months' bank statements
- Form 16 / IT Returns (2 years)
- Employment letter

### For Self-Employed:
- PAN Card & Aadhaar Card
- Business registration proof
- IT Returns (2-3 years)
- Balance sheet & P&L statement
- Last 12 months' bank statements

## Tips to Increase Loan Eligibility

1. **Repay existing debts** before applying
2. **Add a co-applicant** (spouse/parent) to increase eligibility
3. **Avoid multiple loan applications** – each inquiry reduces your CIBIL score
4. **Increase down payment** – higher down payment = lower LTV = better rates
