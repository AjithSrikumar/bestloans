## What Your EMI Actually Consists Of

Each home loan EMI has two parts: the **interest component** and the **principal repayment component**. In the early years, the split is predominantly interest — in Year 1 of a 20-year loan, about 80%–85% of each EMI goes toward interest. Over time, as the principal reduces, the interest share falls and the principal share rises. This is called **amortisation**.

---

## The EMI Formula

```
EMI = P × R × (1 + R)^N ÷ [(1 + R)^N – 1]
```

Where:
- **P** = principal (loan amount)
- **R** = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
- **N** = total number of months

## 5 Key Insights From EMI Calculations

1. **A 0.25% rate drop** on Rs. 50 lakh, 20 years: saves Rs. 854/month and **Rs. 2.05 lakh in total interest**

2. **Extending tenure from 15 to 20 years** (Rs. 50 lakh, 7.25%): saves Rs. 6,034/month EMI but costs **Rs. 10.6 lakh extra in total interest**

3. **30-year tenure trap:** on Rs. 50 lakh at 7.25%, the 30-year total interest (Rs. 69 lakh) **exceeds the original loan amount**

4. **Pre-payment impact:** a Rs. 2 lakh lump sum in Year 3 on a Rs. 30 lakh, 20-year loan at 7.25% reduces tenure by about 14 months and saves **Rs. 1.8 lakh in interest**

5. **Tax savings:** under the old regime, Rs. 2 lakh annual interest deduction at 30% slab = **Rs. 60,000 annual tax saving** — this effectively reduces the EMI cost
