## What is a Joint Home Loan?

A joint home loan is taken by two or more individuals who share both the loan liability and — if they are co-owners — the tax benefits. In 2026, it is one of the most powerful financial strategies for Indian homebuyers:

- Increases loan eligibility by **50–80%**
- Reduces the interest rate when a woman is the primary applicant
- Nearly doubles the household's annual tax deduction

---

## Who Can Be a Co-Applicant in 2026?

| Co-Applicant | Lender Acceptance |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Spouse | Most preferred — accepted by all lenders |
| Parents (father or mother) | Commonly accepted |
| Siblings (brother) | Accepted at most banks |
| Adult children (son or daughter) | Accepted for multi-generational purchases |
| NRI co-applicant | Accepted — income assessed from foreign documents |
| Friends / unrelated partners | Generally NOT accepted |

---

## How Joint Loans Increase Loan Eligibility

Banks calculate eligibility using FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) — typically **40–55% of combined gross monthly income**.

**Example:**
- You earn Rs. 70,000/month
- Your spouse earns Rs. 50,000/month
- Combined Rs. 1,20,000 can support an EMI of Rs. 54,000–66,000
- This translates to a loan of **Rs. 55–68 lakh at 7.5% for 20 years**
- Versus only Rs. 30–38 lakh on a solo loan

## Women Co-Applicant: The 0.05% Rate Concession

In April 2026, nearly all major lenders — SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Bank of Baroda, PNB, Kotak Mahindra Bank — offer a **0.05% (5 bps) interest rate concession** when a woman is the primary applicant or first co-applicant.

On a Rs. 50 lakh loan over 20 years, 0.05% saves approximately **Rs. 30,000** in total interest.

## Tax Strategy: How Both Co-Owners Claim Deductions

Both co-applicants who are also co-owners can each claim independently under the old tax regime:

| Deduction | Each Co-Owner | Combined Annual |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Section 24(b) — Interest | Rs. 2,00,000 | Rs. 4,00,000 |
| Section 80C — Principal | Rs. 1,50,000 | Rs. 3,00,000 |
| **Total** | **Rs. 3,50,000** | **Rs. 7,00,000** |

At a 30% tax bracket for both: annual tax saving of **Rs. 2,10,000** — nearly Rs. 42 lakh saved over a 20-year loan tenure.

---

## Stamp Duty Benefit for Women Co-Owners

Most Indian states offer a **1–2% stamp duty concession** when the property is registered in a woman's name or when a woman is a co-owner:

- **Maharashtra:** 1% concession — on a Rs. 70 lakh property, saves Rs. 70,000
- **Delhi:** 2% concession — on Rs. 1 crore property, saves Rs. 2,00,000
- **UP, Rajasthan, Punjab:** 1% concession

This makes adding a woman co-owner a **triple benefit**: lower rate + lower stamp duty + double tax deduction.

---

## Co-Applicant Credit Score: The Critical Check

In 2026, lenders evaluate the credit profile of **all co-applicants equally**. If your CIBIL is 800 but your co-applicant's is 620, the bank may reject the application or significantly raise the interest rate.

**Ensure both co-applicants have CIBIL scores of 700+ (ideally 750+) before applying jointly.**
