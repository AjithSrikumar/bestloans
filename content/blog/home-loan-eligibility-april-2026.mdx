## The 5 Pillars of Home Loan Eligibility in 2026

Banks and HFCs in April 2026 use five core parameters to decide how much home loan you qualify for: **age, income, CIBIL score, FOIR, and LTV**. Understanding each helps you plan better and avoid surprises at sanction.

---

## 1. Age — Eligibility Windows

| Borrower Type | Minimum Age | Maximum Age (at maturity) |
|--------------|-------------|--------------------------|
| Salaried | 21 years | 65 years |
| Self-employed / Business | 23 years | 70 years |

> Adding a younger co-applicant can extend the eligible tenure, which directly increases the eligible loan amount.

---

## 2. Income Requirements — April 2026

| Profile | Minimum Income |
|---------|---------------|
| Salaried (general) | Rs. 20,000–25,000 net/month |
| Salaried (metro cities — Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru) | Rs. 30,000+ net/month |
| Self-employed | Rs. 2 lakh+ net annual profit (3 years continuity) |

**Sample eligibility at Rs. 50,000 net/month (CIBIL 750+, no existing EMIs):**
- Solo application: Rs. 28–35 lakh for 20-year tenure
- With co-applicant at Rs. 50,000/month: Rs. 56–70 lakh

---

## 3. CIBIL Score — Rate Slabs in April 2026

| Score Band | Rate Range | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 800–900 (Excellent) | 7.10%–7.25% | Fastest approval, best LTV, pre-approved offers possible |
| 750–799 (Very Good) | 7.25%–7.60% | Smooth approval, standard LTV |
| 700–749 (Good) | 7.60%–8.25% | Approved at most banks; additional docs may be requested |
| 650–699 (Fair) | 8.25%–9.50%+ | Limited to NBFCs/HFCs; higher down payment |
| Below 650 | High risk | Most lenders reject — improve score before applying |

---

## 4. FOIR — Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio

**FOIR = (All existing EMIs + Proposed new EMI) ÷ Gross monthly income**

- Acceptable range: **40–55%** for most lenders
- Some allow up to 65% for very high-income borrowers

> **Key insight:** clearing a Rs. 5,000/month personal loan EMI before applying can increase your eligible home loan amount by Rs. 4–6 lakh.

---

## 5. LTV Ratio — How Much Will the Bank Finance?

| Loan Amount | Max LTV | Min Down Payment |
|------------|---------|-----------------|
| Up to Rs. 30 lakh | **90%** | 10% |
| Rs. 30–75 lakh | **80%** | 20% |
| Above Rs. 75 lakh | **75%** | 25% |

---

## How to Increase Your Eligible Amount Before Applying

1. **Pay off personal loans and credit card dues** — reduces FOIR
2. **Add a co-applicant with income and good CIBIL** — increases combined eligibility 50–80%
3. **Improve CIBIL score** — moving from 720 to 750 unlocks better rate slabs
4. **Opt for a longer tenure** — increases eligibility but raises total interest cost
5. **Reduce LTV by increasing down payment** — at 75% LTV, you get better terms
