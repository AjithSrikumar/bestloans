## What is a Home Loan Amortisation Schedule?

An amortisation schedule is a detailed table showing how each of your home loan EMIs is split between **interest and principal** for every month of the loan tenure. Banks are required to provide this schedule at disbursement and make it available on their online portals. Understanding it is essential for making smart prepayment decisions.

---

## How the Interest-Principal Split Changes Over Time

For a **Rs. 50 lakh loan at 7.25% for 20 years (EMI = Rs. 39,672):**

| Month/Year | Interest Portion | Principal Portion |
|------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Month 1** | Rs. 30,208 (76.2%) | Rs. 9,464 (23.8%) |
| **Year 10** | Rs. 19,476 (49.1%) | Rs. 20,196 (50.9%) |
| **Year 18** | Rs. 6,523 (16.4%) | Rs. 33,149 (83.6%) |

This explains why **prepaying in the early years has a dramatically larger impact** than prepaying later — early prepayment cuts high-interest months, while late prepayment cuts mostly principal months.

## How to Use Your Amortisation Schedule for Smart Prepayment

**Identify the breakeven year:** This is when principal exceeds interest in each EMI — typically around Year 8–10. Prepaying before this year saves the most.

**Calculate interest saved:** Your schedule shows how much total interest remains; subtract the same calculation after a prepayment to see exact savings.

**Plan annual prepayments:** Your amortisation schedule shows the outstanding balance at any point — use it to plan how much lump-sum you need to reduce tenure by 2–3 years.
